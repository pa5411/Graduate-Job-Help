General Video Recording Tips

  • If there is both a video and situational assessment, use a device with a keyboard to complete written tasks more easily.
  • Always wear formal clothes unless explicitly told otherwise.
  • Wear respectable trousers/skirts etc in case the recording device is accidentally moved or repositioned.
  • Make sure the room is well-lit so that the assessor can see your eyes clearly.
  • Avoid distracting objects in the background such as posters. Use a clear background such as an empty wall instead.
  • Avoid distracting noises in the background such as phone notifications.
  • Place pets such as cats and dogs in a room different to the room where the recording is to take place.
  • Ensure that the recording device is fixed on a flat surface so that it cannot move during the assessment.
  • Charge any devices before you begin the assessment.
  • Practice recording videos on the device to be used during the assessment before you begin the assessment.
  • Become familiar with the recording software and its settings before the assessment.
  • Look directly into the webcam when speaking.
  • If glasses are worn, then limit glare from the device display by reducing its brightness setting. Ensure that the glasses are clean.
  • Avoid looking into the webcam from unusual angles. The entire face should be visible and fill a good portion of the frame.
  • Avoid talking too quickly or too slowly. Take pauses where necessary.
